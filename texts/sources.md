# Enchiridion — Source Library

URLs for primary texts in the curriculum, organized by period. Sources are primarily
[Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org) (free EPUB/HTML) and
[Internet Archive](https://archive.org) (scans/PDFs). MIT Classics and university-hosted
PDFs fill gaps where Gutenberg lacks a text. [Z-Library](https://www.z-lib.sk) can be used to potentially find EPUBs and PDFs of missing texts as long as they are public domain.

Texts already downloaded into the library are marked **[in repo]**.

## Notes on Missing / Hard-to-Find Texts

### No free English translation available
- **Kepler** — *Astronomia Nova*: Only English translation is Donahue (Green Lion Press, 1992/2015), under copyright. Latin original freely available on Internet Archive. *Harmonies* Book V available in English via Sacred Texts link above.
- **Fibonacci** — *Liber Abaci*: Only English translation is Sigler (Springer, 2002), under copyright. Latin original (Boncompagni 1857 ed.) in repo.
- **Bernoulli, Jakob** — *Ars Conjectandi*: Only English translation is Sylla (Johns Hopkins, 2006), under copyright. Latin original freely available on Internet Archive.
- **Cardano** — *Ars Magna*: Witmer translation (MIT Press, 1968) under copyright; borrow-only on Archive. *Liber de Ludo Aleae*: No free English translation found.
- **Galois** — Mathematical writings: French originals at https://archive.org/details/oeuvres-galois. No authorized free English translation.
- **Gauss** — *Disquisitiones Arithmeticae*: Clarke translation (Yale, 1965) under copyright. Partial English on Wikisource; Latin original free on Archive.
- **Gödel** — "On Formally Undecidable Propositions" (1931): Standard translations (Davis/van Heijenoort) under copyright. A free English translation by Martin Hirzel is hosted at IBM Research (see Section 8).
- **Frege** — *Begriffsschrift*: English translation (Bauer-Mengelberg, in van Heijenoort 1967) under copyright. German original on Gutenberg #48312.
- **Viète** — *The Analytic Art*: Witmer translation (Dover) under copyright. Latin *Opera Mathematica* at https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_25iHrtJpy9oC.
- **Wallis** — *Arithmetica Infinitorum*: Stedall translation (Springer, 2004) under copyright. Latin original at https://archive.org/details/ArithmeticaInfinitorum. Key results in Smith's *Source Book*.
- **Nicole Oresme** — Scientific works: Grant and Menut translations (1960s–70s) under copyright. No free English editions.

### No free English translation available (physics)
- **Planck** — Original quantum hypothesis paper (1900): No free English translation. Nobel lecture (Gutenberg #33663) serves as substitute.
- **Heisenberg** — Matrix mechanics / uncertainty principle papers: No reliable free English translations available.
- **Schrödinger** — Wave equation papers (1926): Collected papers volume on Internet Archive may be borrow-only. Individual papers lack free English translations.
- **Boltzmann** — Statistical mechanics papers: German originals only. Gibbs' *Elementary Principles* (1902, Internet Archive) covers similar ground.

### No free version available (biology & chemistry)
- **Jacob & Monod** — Gene regulation / operon model (1961): Elsevier copyright.
- **Hamilton** — "The Genetical Evolution of Social Behaviour" (1964): Elsevier copyright.
- **Pauling** — *The Nature of the Chemical Bond* (book, 1939): Under copyright. Nobel lecture (1954) free at nobelprize.org.
- **Carson** — *Silent Spring* (1962): Under copyright until ~2058.
